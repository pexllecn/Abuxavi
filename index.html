Finding your week 1 loads

No guessing and no ego. In week 1, for every lift, pick a weight where you finish the top of the rep range feeling you had 2 reps left in the tank. That weight is your start. Write it down. From week 2, progression takes over.

Progression · where muscle is built

Double progression. Example, Bench 4 × 6 to 8 starting at 60 kg:

WeekSetsRead

Once every set hits the top of the range, add 2.5 kg (5 kg on leg press and hip thrust) and build the reps back up. That is the whole game.

Focus

The next 90 days

Four priorities. Nothing else matters until these are automatic.
Priority 1 · Show up

Five sessions a week, every week. Motivation is irrelevant. At 26.7% fat the single biggest lever is not missing. Khaled trains whether he feels like it or not. So do you now.

Priority 2 · Protein and steps, daily

200g protein and 8–10k steps. Hit these two on a bad day and the bad day still counts. Miss them on a perfect gym day and the day is wasted.

Priority 3 · Sleep 7.5 hours

A metabolic age of 51 at 36 years old is partly recovery debt. Sleep is when fat loss and muscle repair are signed off. In bed by 23:00.

Priority 4 · Get stronger anyway

At your body fat you can build strength while losing weight. If your lifts climb while the scale drops from 95.3 toward 88, you are doing a near perfect cut. Chase both.

No bulking. No crash dieting. No new plans. No random cheat weekends. One plate, one split, one direction until the August milestone. Then we review.
