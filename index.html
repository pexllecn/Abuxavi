Training reference

tap to open
Warm up first
  • 5 min easy on the bike or treadmill to raise the temperature.
  • Ramp the first big lift: empty bar, ~50%, ~70%, ~85%. Never hit a top set cold.
  • Lower: leg swings, bodyweight squats, glute bridges, hip 90/90.
  • Upper: band pull-aparts, dislocates, scapular push-ups.
Progression · reps then weight
Add reps before weight. Take one set up a rep each week until every set sits at the top of the range, then add 2.5kg and start again. Bench, top of a 4×5–6:
Week 1
95×595×595×595×5
Week 2
95×695×595×595×5
Week 3
95×695×695×595×5
Week 4
95×695×695×695×6
+
All four at the top, so next week is 97.5kg × 5 and it repeats. Same rule on your 3-set lifts.
Weekly split · 5 train, 2 rest
Cardio & recovery
15-min incline walk × 3, straight after the weights on both lower days and Saturday. 5–6 km/h at 10–15% incline. That's the only cardio. If the scale stalls two weeks, add a fourth walk before touching the food.
  • Sleep 7.5h, fixed wake time. The biggest lever left.
  • Last caffeine early afternoon, screens down before bed.
  • Pre-log the day each morning, train the evening with Mufleh.
Scale rules
  • Weigh daily, first thing, same conditions. Act on the 7-day average only.
  • Around 0.5kg down hold everything. Faster than 0.8, eat a little more.
  • Flat for two weeks, drop midweek carbs or add the fourth walk.
  • Sub-15%, ease the deficit to protect muscle. Ignore the scale 2–3 days after Saturday.
Abs & cramps
Abs come last. Hanging Leg Raise ends Lower A, Cable Crunch ends Lower B. They show up when the fat drops, not from extra crunches.
Lying leg press cramp: knees out of your chest, pelvis down
Breathe out as you press. Salt before training, 300–400mg magnesium at night, keep carbs on lifting days. Keep 1–2 reps in the tank on compounds, sharp pain stops, burn doesn't.
Straps & gear
Straps for RDLs, rows, pulldowns. Belt for top sets of squat, hip thrust, OHP. Wrist wraps for heavy press, knee sleeves for warmth.
